**Juthoor LV1 — Cross-Scholar Agreement Analysis**
Date: 2026-03-26

---

## 1. Overview

Four primary scholars are compared in the LV1 genome: Jabal (المعجم الاشتقاقي), Hassan Abbas, Asim al-Masri, and Neili. A fifth scholar, Anbar, contributes supplementary readings. This report covers 30 letter entries: the 28 standard Arabic consonants plus two variants included by Abbas and Asim (ألف المد) and Anbar's entry for الفتحة.

Agreement levels are defined as:
- **STRONG** — 2+ features shared across 2+ scholars (cross-validated phonosemantic content)
- **PARTIAL** — 1 feature shared, or clear synonym-level agreement across 2+ scholars
- **DIVERGE** — no shared features despite 2+ scholars covering the letter; or each scholar measures a different dimension of the same articulation

## 3. Top 5 Most Agreed Letters

These are the letters with the strongest cross-scholar confirmation — two or more features validated independently by multiple sources.

### 3.1 خ — Rarefying Exit (Highest Cross-Scholar Confirmation)

- Jabal: تخلخل، جفاف، غلظ
- Abbas: تخلخل، جفاف، خروج
- Asim: خروج، انتقال

Three features confirmed across pairings: تخلخل + جفاف (Jabal ↔ Abbas), خروج (Asim ↔ Abbas). No other letter in the dataset achieves three independently confirmed features. The خاء is the "dry, loosening exit." All three scholars, different frameworks, same result.

**Consensus features:** تخلخل + جفاف + خروج

### 3.2 ص — Dense Pure Void (Triple Feature Match)

- Jabal: نفاذ، غلظ، خلوص، فراغ
- Abbas: غلظ، خلوص، فراغ
- Asim: انتقال (no overlap)

غلظ + خلوص + فراغ: a three-feature exact match between Jabal and Abbas. This is the deepest lexical agreement in the dataset for a two-scholar pair. Asim contributes nothing here but the Jabal-Abbas triad is the most confident reading in LV1.

**Consensus features:** غلظ + خلوص + فراغ

### 3.3 ذ — Unanimous Penetration

- Jabal: نفاذ، رخاوة، غلظ
- Asim: نفاذ، انتقال، التحام
- Abbas: نفاذ

All three scholars agree on نفاذ. Abbas's mechanism is articulatory mimesis: the tongue slides between the teeth = penetration. This is textbook phonosemantic confirmation. نفاذ for ذ is the most certain single-feature reading in the dataset.

**Consensus feature:** نفاذ (unanimous)

### 3.4 س — Flowing Extension

- Jabal: امتداد، دقة، وحدة، حدة
- Asim: امتداد، ظهور
- Abbas: دقة، امتداد

All three agree on امتداد. Jabal and Abbas also agree on دقة. The sustained sibilant س embodies linear outward flow. Two features, three sources. This is the most reliable double confirmation after ذ.

**Consensus features:** امتداد + دقة

### 3.5 ك — Double Independent Pair

- Jabal: ضغط، دقة، امتساك، قطع
- Asim: تجمع، احتواء
- Abbas: ضغط، تماسك
- Neili: تجمع

ك achieves an unusual four-scholar confirmation via two independent pairings: ضغط (Jabal ↔ Abbas) and تجمع (Asim ↔ Neili). These two features are not contradictory — they may describe different aspects of the velar stop's articulatory effect: cohesive gathering and focused pressure simultaneously.

**Consensus features:** ضغط (Jabal-Abbas validated) + تجمع (Asim-Neili validated)

## 4. The 5 Priority DIVERGE Letters

These five letters represent divergences with theoretical significance beyond data quality issues.

### 4.1 ز — Methodological Incompatibility

| Scholar | Reading | Method |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Jabal | اكتناز، ازدحام | Lexical pattern analysis |
| Abbas | تخلخل | Auditory/sensory mimesis |
| Asim | انتقال | Kinetic intentionality |
| Anbar | احتكاك، التحام | Phonetic class analysis |

Jabal says dense packing and crowding. Abbas says rarefaction and vibration. These are near-antonyms. The divergence is not a data error — it is methodological. Abbas is describing the auditory quality of the voiced buzzing sibilant (the buzz = vibration = تخلخل). Jabal is describing the lexical patterns of roots containing ز (which cluster around crowding and density). They are measuring different things.

This is the clearest case in the dataset where lexical-pattern analysis and sensory-mimetic analysis produce contradictory outputs for the same letter. The divergence cannot be resolved by normalization; it requires a theoretical decision about which method should govern the scoring engine.

### 4.2 و — Hollow Letter with Near-Zero Lexical Weight

| Scholar | Reading | Note |
|---------|---------|------|
| Jabal | اشتمال، احتواء | Lexical |
| Abbas | تلاصق | يقيناً — but notes "شبه معدوم" lexical influence |
| Asim | حيز، انتقال | Spatial location |
| Anbar | تجمع | Bilabial rounding |

الواو is one of the حروف الجوفية — the "hollow letters" that function primarily as vowel extensions and grammatical connectors in Classical Arabic. Abbas explicitly notes that و's independent contribution to root meaning is near-zero. The scholars disagree because they are trying to assign semantic weight to a letter that may not carry independent meaning in the lexical system. This divergence may be expected and acceptable: the prediction engine should simply assign و a low weight regardless of which reading is selected.

### 4.3 ي — Hollow Letter, Four Directional Readings

| Scholar | Reading | Note |
|---------|---------|------|
| Jabal | اتصال، وحدة، تفرق، رقة | Continuity/connection |
| Abbas | باطن | يقيناً — downward direction |
| Asim | تعلق، انتقال | Temporal persistence |
| Neili | امتداد | Extension/continuity |
| Anbar | نقص | Gravitational descent |

Like و, the ياء is a حرف جوفي. There is a soft cluster around continuity (Jabal's اتصال, Asim's تعلق, Neili's امتداد) but Abbas and Anbar read a different spatial-directional dimension. The divergence is unlikely to have large practical impact given the letter's minimal independent lexical contribution.

### 4.4 ا / ألف — Root vs. Variant Confusion

The standard alif (ء in consonantal position) achieves PARTIAL agreement (تأكيد: Jabal ↔ Abbas). The ألف المد entry — the extended vowel alif included by Abbas and Asim but not covered by Jabal and Neili — has no cross-scholar framework for comparison because only two scholars address it. The divergence here is one of scope: Jabal's system simply does not assign the long vowel alif an independent phonosemantic value. Abbas's يقيناً classification of ألف المد as a vowel-of-direction is internally consistent but cannot be compared against scholars who do not include it.

### 4.5 الفتحة — Anbar-Only Entry

الفتحة (the short-a vowel) appears only in Anbar's analysis as a phonosemantic unit. No other scholar in the dataset assigns independent phonosemantic content to short vowels. This is not a divergence in the ordinary sense — it is a fundamental methodological difference: Anbar's framework extends to vocalic phonemes while all other scholars restrict themselves to consonants. Cross-scholar comparison is not possible for this entry by design.

## 5. Scholar Agreement Rankings

### Most Broadly Aligned: Jabal ↔ Abbas

Jabal and Abbas are the strongest pair in the dataset. At least one shared feature appears in 15 of the 28 consonants. Eight letters achieve STRONG agreement between them. Both scholars work from a materialist decomposition — Jabal from lexical corpus patterns, Abbas from articulatory-sensory mimesis — and their convergence on texture and pressure features (غلظ، جفاف، كثافة، ضغط) reflects real phonosemantic substance confirmed from two independent directions. Abbas should be the first scholar consulted when Jabal's reading requires external validation.

### Closest to Consensus: Abbas (for STRONG letters)

For the 8 STRONG letters, Abbas matches Jabal on the shared features in all 8 cases. His mechanism types (إيمائية in particular) provide articulatory grounding that is independently derivable from phonetic theory, which means his agreements with Jabal are not circular. Abbas is the most reliable cross-validation source in the current dataset.

### Weakest Alignment: Jabal ↔ Asim

Approximately 5 of 28 letters share features at the lexical level. Asim's kinetic-intentionality framework (انبثاق، اندفاع، هيمنة) uses a verbal vocabulary that rarely maps directly onto Jabal's nominal-adjectival features (تخلخل، جفاف، رخاوة). This is partly a vocabulary problem: a normalization pass mapping Asim's kinetic terms to the canonical feature set would likely raise the Jabal-Asim overlap to 10-12 letters.

### Note on Neili

Neili covers only 10 letters and has sparse atomic features (3 letters with empty feature lists). The Asim-Neili surface disagreement (2/10 shared features at lexical level) is a data artifact — Neili's kinetic_gloss descriptions align with Asim's framework, but were captured at a summary level that did not populate the atomic_features field. Neili's data should not be treated as contradicting Asim; it is under-extracted.

## 6. Recommendations for the Scoring Engine

1. **STRONG letters (ح، خ، ذ، س، ص، ض، ق، ك):** Use consensus features as primary scoring atoms with high confidence. These 8 letters have cross-validated phonosemantic content from at least two independent scholars.

2. **PARTIAL letters (ء، ب، ث، ج، ش، ط، ظ، غ، ف، ل، ن، ر، ألف المد):** Retain Jabal as primary. Mark the confirmed shared feature as "cross-validated" in the scoring system — it can carry a confidence bonus in nucleus predictions.

3. **DIVERGE letters — priority review:**
   - **ز:** Flag as methodological divergence. Prefer Jabal's lexical reading (اكتناز/ازدحام) for the scoring engine as it is derived from corpus evidence rather than auditory mimesis.
   - **و / ي:** Assign reduced weight regardless of reading. Abbas's "شبه معدوم" judgment about their lexical contribution is the key constraint.
   - **ا/ألف المد:** Accept Abbas's يقيناً entry as the working reading; note no Jabal comparison is possible.
   - **الفتحة:** Exclude from consonant-based scoring; Anbar's vowel-phoneme analysis operates in a different framework.

4. **Abbas as primary validation scholar:** The Jabal-Abbas alignment (15/28 letters, 8 STRONG) is the empirical backbone of the cross-scholar confirmation system. Abbas's يقيناً entries for the hollow letters and his sensory-mimetic mechanism types provide the most methodologically independent confirmation available.
