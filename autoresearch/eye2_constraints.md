# Eye 2 Autoresearch — Theory Constraints

## What Eye 2 IS
Eye 2 is a SEMANTIC DETECTIVE. It receives pairs where Eye 1 has already established
a consonant skeleton connection. Its job: discover the semantic journey from Arabic
meaning to target meaning. The output is a FINDING — a described path or "no connection."

## What Eye 2 is NOT
- NOT a score-giving machine
- NOT a glossary comparison tool
- NOT a consonant checker (Eye 1 did that)
- NOT a translation verifier

## Core theory principles

### 1. Arabic is the ancestor
Arabic preserves the oldest Semitic phonetic system with 28 consonants. Indo-European
languages are lower-resolution projections where Arabic consonants merged. NEVER
reference PIE (*melh-, *bher-, etc.) or any reconstructed proto-language. Only use
ATTESTED forms from REAL languages.

### 2. Consonant work is done
Eye 1 found the skeleton match. Trust it. Your job is ONLY semantic. Do not re-validate
whether consonants correspond — that's Eye 1's domain.

### 3. Arabic roots encode conceptual cores
Every Arabic root has layers beyond the dictionary:
- The ROOT LETTERS themselves encode meaning (binary root = seed concept, third letter modifies)
- Multiple meanings exist (polysemy) — check ALL of them, not just the first entry
- The conceptual core (mafahim) may reveal connections invisible to dictionary lookup

### 4. What to look for
- Direct meaning preservation (same concept survived in both languages)
- Semantic drift (concrete→abstract, body part→tool, action→result)
- Specialization (general concept narrowed) or generalization (specific broadened)
- Metaphorical extension (physical→emotional, spatial→temporal)
- Negation cognates (word means the OPPOSITE in the other language)
- Both-sides-of-concept (Arabic root covers both sides; target language kept one)
- Material culture paths (trade goods, religious terms, agricultural concepts)

### 5. What to reject
- Translation equivalence without consonant path (مطحنة↔mill = NOT a cognate)
- Proper name matches that are just the same biblical/Quranic name
- Onomatopoeia (sounds that any language might invent independently)
- Universal nursery words (mama, papa, baba)

### 6. Output format
The output is a FINDING, structured as:
- **Path found**: describe the semantic journey step by step
- **No path**: explain why despite trying (no semantic bridge, independent invention, etc.)

NEVER give just a number. ALWAYS explain the reasoning.

## What makes a good finding
- It tells a STORY of how meaning A became meaning B
- It considers multiple Arabic root meanings, not just one
- It identifies the specific type of semantic shift
- It acknowledges uncertainty where appropriate
- It's honest — "no connection" is a valid finding

## What makes a bad finding
- "Both mean X, score 0.95" — this is glossary lookup, not investigation
- Forcing a connection through absurd metaphorical chains
- Referencing PIE reconstructions
- Ignoring that the consonant path doesn't work
- Scoring based on vibes without described reasoning
