## THE JUTHOOR THESIS
Arabic preserves the oldest Semitic phonetic system (28+ consonants). When words traveled from Arabic/Semitic into Greek, Latin, and other languages, consonants merged but MEANINGS persisted. Your task is to find these hidden semantic links.

## HOW ARABIC ROOTS CARRY MEANING
Every Arabic root has layers:
- MASADIQ (dictionary): surface definitions — "كلب = dog"
- MAFAHIM (genome): the conceptual CORE — "كلب = biting/seizing with force"
- The ROOT LETTERS themselves encode meaning: the first two letters (binary root) carry the seed concept, the third letter modifies it

When the Arabic meaning is given in Arabic script, READ IT DIRECTLY — you understand Arabic.

## HOW TO SCORE
For each pair, ask THREE questions:
1. Does the DICTIONARY meaning (masadiq) of the Arabic word overlap with the {LANG_NAME} word? → masadiq_direct
2. Does the CONCEPTUAL CORE (mafahim) connect, even if dictionaries don't show it? → mafahim_deep
3. Could the Arabic meaning have evolved into the {LANG_NAME} meaning through metaphor, specialization, or generalization? → combined

## POLYSEMY RULE

## MULTI-STEP CHAINS
Trace etymological paths up to 5 steps through intermediate languages. Arabic → Aramaic → Greek → Latin → French → English is a common path.

Example: جُبّة (cloak) → Latin cappa (cape) → capella (short cloak/shrine) → chapel (4 steps)

Do NOT limit yourself to 1-2 steps. Real etymologies often need 3-5 steps. If you can trace a plausible multi-step chain, score accordingly (0.6-0.75 range).

## PREFIX MATCHING
Arabic functional words can correspond to Greek/Latin prefixes:
- Arabic بدون (without) = Greek ἀ-/ἀν- = Latin in-/im-/dis-
- Arabic فوق (above) = Greek ὑπέρ- = Latin super-
- Arabic تحت (under) = Greek ὑπο- = Latin sub-

When scoring, strip negation/location prefixes and compare the ROOT forms. A match at the root level with prefix correspondence is a valid cognate pattern.

## DERIVATION FAMILIES
If the target word belongs to a word family, check whether the Arabic root matches ANY member of that family:
- "chapel, chaplain, chaperon, cap, cape" all derive from Latin cappa
- Arabic جُبّة matches cappa (cloak), therefore it matches the whole family

When you see a target word, mentally expand it to its broader derivation family before scoring.

## SEMANTIC DRIFT PATTERNS TO LOOK FOR
- Body part → tool/vessel (يد hand → handle)
- Action → result of action (كتب to write → book/scripture)
- Concrete → abstract (ثقل heavy → dignity/authority)
- Specific → general or vice versa (كلب dog → canine/any predator)
- Object → quality (ذهب gold → to go/depart — shared root concept of "departure/precious")
- Both sides of a concept (إثم sin ↔ θέμις divine law = moral order)
- Agent → place/instrument (حكم to judge → tribunal)
- Negation cognates (أفنى perish ↔ ἄφθιτον imperishable — OPPOSITE but same root concept)
